Overview
The Full Plan
Two-week roadmap · Apr 18 → May 3
The core problem to solve: Afternoon / Day 2 energy crashes in club volleyball almost always come from three compounding failures — (1) missing the post-match fueling window, (2) chronic mild dehydration starting the night before, and (3) glycogen stores that were never fully loaded in the days prior. Fix those three things and the wall largely disappears.
Phase 1 · Apr 18–25
Two Weeks Out — Build the Base
  • Whole food carbs at every meal — rice, oats, sweet potato, whole grain bread
  • Protein target ~0.7–0.8g per lb bodyweight daily
  • Start batch cooking staples: overnight oats, chicken, rice, eggs, energy balls
  • Hydration baseline: half bodyweight in oz/day as floor; electrolytes 1–2×/week
  • 9–10 hrs sleep; consistent wake time; foam roll before bed
  • Cut ultra-processed foods, excessive sugar, fast food — now, not week-of
Phase 2 · Apr 26 – May 1
One Week Out — Load & Saturate
  • Increase complex carbs 10–20% starting Wednesday — fills muscle glycogen
  • No new foods rule — stick entirely to what her gut already knows
  • Daily electrolyte drink every practice day (not just post-practice)
  • Add coconut water as a natural potassium source
  • Daily 15-min stretch: dynamic morning, static post-practice, foam roll evening
  • Reduce cruciferous veg, fried food, and high-fiber foods Thu–Fri to avoid GI issues
  • Brief her on the 2–3pm cortisol dip — fuel before it, not after
Phase 3 · Fri May 1
Day Before — Noon is the Fuel Load
  • Carb-rich breakfast: oatmeal + eggs + OJ
  • Noon lunch is the primary carb-load — chicken pasta, generous portion
  • Lighter dinner done by 6:30–7pm (chicken rice bowl)
  • Tart cherry juice with dinner Friday night
  • Bag fully packed by 9pm · No screens after 9:30pm · Bed by 9:30–10pm
Phase 4 · Sat May 2 · 3pm Start
Day 1 — Late Start Advantage
  • Full morning to hydrate and top off glycogen — use it
  • Noon meal: tournament rice bowl (last real meal before competing)
  • 2:00pm: banana or dates only — no solid food after
  • Post-match refuel within 15 min — every single match, no exceptions
  • Late-evening energy trap: don't skip post-match fuel because dinner feels close
Between Days · Sat Night
Saturday Night — This Night IS Day 2
  • Recovery dinner (protein-forward) within 60–90 min of leaving venue
  • Tart cherry juice at dinner — reduces overnight muscle soreness measurably
  • 30 min recovery: foam roll, ice hot spots, static stretch — do it tonight
  • 9pm bedtime target — 8am Sunday means 5:30–6am wake-up
  • Prep Sunday breakfast and pack bag tonight — zero friction in the morning
Phase 5 · Sun May 3 · 8am Start
Day 2 — Early Morning Urgency
  • Wake 5:30–6am · Eat by 5:45am — non-negotiable
  • Overnight oats prepped Saturday night — open fridge, eat, done
  • Electrolyte + sea salt water at breakfast — muscles need it more today
  • Beet shot 2–3 hrs before first match (optional, strong evidence)
  • Longer warm-up (15 min) — body is stiff from Day 1
  • Proactive snack at 2pm before the cortisol dip — not after

Batch Cook
Weekly Batch Staples
Cook once on Sunday — feeds every meal in the plan. ~90 min total, mostly hands-off.
How to use this: Make all 6 items on Sunday or Monday. Everything keeps 5 days refrigerated. They aren't just standalone meals — they're building blocks pulled across every breakfast, lunch, and tournament day. Make a double batch of energy balls and freeze half.
Batch #1 · 15 min
Overnight Oats × 5
One mason jar per day — grab and go. No morning cooking.
  • ½ cup rolled oats per jar
  • ½ cup Greek yogurt per jar
  • ½ cup milk (any kind)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • ¼ tsp vanilla extract
  • Banana + PB — add morning of
Method: Layer in jar, stir, lid on, fridge overnight. In morning: slice banana on top, add 1 tbsp peanut butter. Keeps 5 days. Make a larger jar for Sunday morning itself.
Batch #2 · 40 min total
Honey Garlic Chicken
2 lbs — goes in bowls, wraps, or straight from the container all week.
  • 2 lbs boneless chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce or coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder
Method: Marinate 15 min. Bake at 400°F for 22–25 min. Slice and refrigerate. Reheat in microwave 90 sec. Use in bowls, wraps, rice plates.
Batch #3 · 25 min total
White Rice (6 cups cooked)
The neutral base for everything. Reheats in 60 seconds.
  • 2 cups dry white rice
  • 3 cups water + ½ tsp salt
  • Optional: 1 tsp butter or olive oil
Method: Rinse rice. Boil water. Add rice, reduce to low, cover 18 min. Fluff. Portion into containers. Fridge 5 days, freezer 3 months.
Batch #4 · 12 min total
Hard-Boiled Eggs × 12
Most portable protein on the planet. Pre-peel and bag for tournament day.
  • 12 large eggs
  • 1 tsp salt (for water)
  • Ice bath ready
Method: Cold eggs into boiling salted water. 11 minutes exactly. Ice bath 5 min. Store unpeeled up to 7 days. Peel tournament batch the morning before leaving.
Batch #5 · 15 min + chill / Freeze 20+
Energy Balls
Pre/between-match fuel. 2–3 per serving. Double the batch and freeze half.
  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • ½ cup peanut or almond butter
  • ⅓ cup honey
  • ¼ cup mini chocolate chips
  • ¼ cup ground flaxseed
  • 1 tsp vanilla · Pinch of salt
Method: Mix all. Chill 30 min. Roll into 1" balls. Freeze on a sheet, then bag. Keep in cooler for tournament days. Makes ~22 balls.
Batch #6 · 10 min / Fridge 5 days
Cut Fruit Container
Fast carbs + hydration. More effective between matches than it sounds.
  • ½ watermelon, cubed
  • 2 cups grapes
  • 3 oranges, segmented
  • 1 cup strawberries, halved
  • Squeeze of lime to prevent browning
Method: Cut, toss with lime juice, seal airtight. Keeps 4–5 days. Pack directly into tournament cooler — watermelon chunks are especially hydrating.

Monday & Thursday Dinner · 25 min
Pasta with Meat Sauce
The classic carb-load anchor. Make it twice — portions up Thursday.
High CarbHigh ProteinBatch Friendly

  • Olive oil1 tbsp

Wednesday Dinner · 35 min
Sheet Pan Chicken + Sweet Potato
One pan, no mess — sweet potato is a glycogen superstar.
High CarbHigh ProteinOne Pan
  • Chicken thighs, boneless4 pieces
  • Sweet potatoes, cubed2 large
  • Green beans2 cups
  • Olive oil3 tbsp
  • Garlic powder, paprika, onion powder1 tsp each
  • Salt and pepperTo taste
  • Lemon, halved1
  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan.
  2. Toss sweet potato cubes in 1 tbsp oil + half the spices. Spread on pan. Roast 15 min.
  3. Flip potatoes. Add green beans and seasoned chicken to pan.
  4. Roast another 20–22 min until chicken reaches 165°F internally.
  5. Squeeze lemon over everything. Serve immediately.

Friday Lunch · PRIMARY FUEL MEAL · 20 min
Olive Oil Chicken Pasta
The main fuel load for Day 1. No heavy sauce — clean carbs and easy digestion.
Primary Carb LoadHigh ProteinEasy Digestion

Hydration Recipes
Homemade Game Day Electrolyte
32 oz water + ¼ tsp sea salt + 1 tbsp honey + juice of ½ lemon. Shake to mix. Costs pennies. Matches store-bought electrolyte profile. Make 2–3 bottles per tournament day. Use as the continuous sipping drink.
Tart Cherry Recovery Drink
8 oz tart cherry juice + 4 oz water + squeeze of lemon + pinch of honey. Serve at dinner Friday night AND Saturday night. Shown in research to reduce next-day muscle soreness and improve sleep quality. Brands: Lakewood, R.W. Knudsen.
Beet Shot — Day 2 Performance
2–3 oz pure beet juice or 1 BeetElite packet in 4 oz water. Take 2–3 hours before first match Sunday. Try it once during the week to confirm no GI reaction. Improves oxygen efficiency and endurance — strong evidence base in teen athletes.

The Non-Negotiables
Rule #1
Post-Match 15-Min Window

Fast carb + protein within 15 minutes of every match ending. Set a phone alarm. She will forget. This single habit is the biggest lever on both afternoon energy and Day 2 performance.

Rule #2
No New Foods Week-Of

No new restaurants, new protein powders, new recipes. GI distress during matches is real and completely avoidable. Every food this week should be something she's eaten before without issue.

Rule #3
Saturday Night Bedtime

9pm target. No screens after 9pm. Everything is prepped before she sits down. A 5:30am wake-up for 8am Day 2 requires it. This single night determines more of Sunday's performance than anything else.
