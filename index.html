Tips
Venue Tips
Things that make tournament day easier
🕐
Arrive Early
Saturday: Aim to arrive by 1:30–2pm for a 3pm first match. Sunday: Arrive by 7:15am for an 8am start. Finding parking and walking to Hall A can take 15–20 minutes alone.
📱
Download Before You Go
Cell service is unreliable in large convention halls with hundreds of people. Download your schedule, court assignments, and any bracket apps before leaving the hotel or car. Screenshot everything.
👟
Comfortable Shoes
GWCC is enormous. Parents and coaches will log several miles across a tournament day walking between halls, parking, and food. Bring comfortable walking shoes.
🌡️
Layer Up
Convention halls run cold when AC is cranked — especially early morning. Players sitting between matches can get chilled fast. A team hoodie or light jacket in the bag is worth it.
🅿️
Plan Parking in Advance
GWCC parking fills fast during large events. Check if online pre-purchase is available via ParkMobile or the GWCC website. Set a meeting point in case your group gets separated.
🍔
CNN Center Food Court
For a better meal than concession food, CNN Center connects to GWCC on the east side and has a food court with more options including Chick-fil-A.
📍
Set a Team Meet Spot
Pick one specific spot as the rally point before each match. Text doesn't always work well inside the halls — have a physical meeting place pre-agreed.
🔋
Charge Everything
Phones, tablets, portable battery packs — charge them all the night before. Two long tournament days will drain batteries. Bring a small power bank.
🏨
Stay Close
The Signia by Hilton connects directly to GWCC Hall C — walking distance with no parking needed Sunday morning. Or look for hotels on the MARTA line to skip parking stress entirely.

The Big 3 Things That Actually Matter
If you do nothing else, do these three things
The real reason teams fade in the afternoon or Day 2: They didn't eat right after matches, they showed up slightly dehydrated, and they didn't sleep enough the night before. You don't need to be perfect — you just need to fix those three things.
The #1 Thing
Eat Within 20 Min After Each Match

Within 20 minutes of a match ending, get carbs and protein into her — banana + string cheese, granola bar + fruit, peanut butter crackers. This is the single biggest lever you have for how she feels during the next match.

The #2 Thing
Hydrate Tonight & Tomorrow Morning

Dehydration doesn't start the day of the tournament — it starts 12–24 hours before. 16–24 oz of water tonight and a full water bottle first thing tomorrow morning will carry her through the morning matches.

The #3 Thing
Sleep 8–9 Hours Before Day 1

Sleep is when muscle glycogen replenishes and the body resets. Late nights Friday night will cost real energy on Saturday afternoon. Phones off, lights out by 9:30–10pm. Nothing about the night before matters more than this.

The Simple Framework: Eat within 20 min of each match ending · Sip water constantly (not guzzle) · Get 8+ hours of sleep before both days · Don't skip breakfast · Keep snacks in the bag at all times

Simple Dinners That Work
Any Night · 25 min
Pasta + Meat Sauce
Make it Monday and again Thursday with bigger portions

Ingredients:

  • Pasta (any shape)12 oz box
  • Ground beef or turkey1 lb
  • Jarred pasta sauce1 jar
  • Garlic powder, Italian seasoning1 tsp each

How to make it:

  1. Boil salted water, cook pasta until done. Drain.
  2. Brown the meat in a pan. Pour in the sauce, simmer 5 min.
  3. Mix with pasta. Top with parmesan. Serve with garlic bread.
Wednesday Night · 30 min
Baked Chicken + Sweet Potato
One pan — sweet potato is one of the best things she can eat this week.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken thighs or breasts4 pieces
  • Sweet potatoes, cubed2 large
  • Olive oil2–3 tbsp
  • Garlic powder, paprika, salt, pepperGenerous

How to make it:

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Toss sweet potato in oil and seasoning, spread on sheet pan.
  2. Roast 15 min, then add seasoned chicken and any veggies to the pan.
  3. Roast another 20–22 min until chicken is cooked through.

Prep Tonight → Ready at 6am
  1. Mix ½ cup oats + ½ cup yogurt + ½ cup milk + honey in a mason jar or container.

Snacks & Drinks
Simple combos that actually work — no specialty store required
Post-Match Snack Combos — Eat Within 15–20 Min
Combo #1 · Easiest
Banana + String Cheese
Pre-peel the banana and put it in a bag before leaving the house. Grab it and a string cheese right after the whistle. 90 seconds to eat. This combo is enough.
Combo #2 · Crunchy
PB Crackers
A pack of peanut butter crackers (Ritz or Lance) + a banana or some fruit. Easy to find at a gas station if you forgot to pack something.
Combo #3 · More Filling
Granola Bar + Fruit
A Chewy, Nature Valley, or RX Bar + an orange or some grapes. Good if there's a longer break coming and she needs something more substantial.
Combo #4 · Refreshing
Watermelon + Yogurt Tube
Watermelon chunks + a squeezable Greek yogurt tube. Great for warm gyms. High water content helps hydration. Easy to eat standing up.
Combo #5 · Make Ahead
Energy Balls
2 cups oats + ½ cup peanut butter + ⅓ cup honey + mini chocolate chips. Mix, chill 30 min, roll into balls, freeze. Grab 2–3 as a quick snack.
Combo #6 · Emergency Backup
RX Bar + Coconut Water
Keep 2–3 buried in the bag. Whole food ingredients. Pair with coconut water (natural electrolytes) if she's really dragging.

Hydration Tips
💧
The Sip Rule
Sipping consistently beats chugging. Chugging causes cramps and a sloshing stomach. One or two sips every few minutes beats drinking a full bottle at once.
🟡
Check the Color
Pale yellow = good. Dark yellow = drink more right now. Clear = over-hydrated (rare). The goal is pale yellow all day long.
🍉
Food Hydrates Too
Watermelon, oranges, grapes, and strawberries are mostly water. Packing fruit isn't just about carbs — it contributes to hydration.
🧂
Salt Matters
She's sweating out sodium. Plain water alone doesn't replace it. Sports drinks or electrolyte packets matter on tournament days — they have salt in them.
⏰
Start Early
It takes hours for hydration to catch up. Starting a water bottle on the car ride to the venue is much more effective than frantically drinking right before a match.
😴
Hydrate Before Bed
Drinking 8–12 oz of water before sleep on Saturday night helps start Day 2 better hydrated. The body keeps using water overnight.

Pro Tips
Weekend Logistics Tips
How to make the Buckhead hotel base work for a 2-day tournament
🛎️
Check In Early
Call (470-231-3030) and request early check-in for Friday. Arriving mid-afternoon means you can settle in, hit CVS for supplies, and have a calm dinner before early bedtime.
🧊
Load the Fridge
Room fridges are your best tool. Hit Whole Foods or CVS on Friday and stock it: bananas, PB, string cheese, overnight oat ingredients, Gatorade. You won't need to hunt for breakfast at 5:30am.
🏊
Pool for Recovery
The heated indoor pool is a legitimate recovery tool. After Day 1 (Saturday), a 10–15 minute soak helps with leg fatigue. Get in around 7pm, out by 8pm, light snack, bed by 10.
🍝
Friday Night Dinner
Don't skip dinner or eat junk Friday. Best move: True Food Kitchen (5-min walk) or Lokma at the Food Hall (inside the hotel complex). Carbs + protein. Eat by 7:30pm.
⛽
Gas Up Before Buckhead
Fill your tank at an I-85 exit or I-285 before reaching Buckhead. Gas in this area runs premium. One less thing to deal with on tournament morning when you're in a hurry.
📍
Pre-Save the Venue
Before leaving Columbus, screenshot the exact venue address from srva.org/regionals and drop a Google Maps pin. Tournament morning is not the time to troubleshoot directions.
🎬
Friday Wind-Down
AMC Phipps Plaza 14 is inside Phipps (connected to the hotel). Good option for a low-energy Friday night decompression. Early show, back by 9–10pm.
🧳
Forgotten Gear? Lenox
Lenox Square is directly across Peachtree Rd (~0.5 mi). If anyone forgets knee pads, athletic wear, or training shoes — Lenox has athletic retailers and department stores.
🚗
Rideshare Is Easy Here
Buckhead is one of Atlanta's best rideshare zones. If venue parking is a concern, Uber/Lyft from the hotel to venue and back is a clean option. Keep the cars at Phipps.
